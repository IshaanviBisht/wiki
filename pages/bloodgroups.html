There are 8 main blood groups, based on the presence or absence of two antigens (A and B) and the Rh factor (+ or -):

1. A+
Has A antigen and Rh factor. Can donate to: A+, AB+. Can receive from: A+, A-, O+, O-

2. A-
Has A antigen, no Rh factor. Can donate to: A+, A-, AB+, AB-. Can receive from: A-, O-

3. B+
Has B antigen and Rh factor. Can donate to: B+, AB+. Can receive from: B+, B-, O+, O-

4. B-
Has B antigen, no Rh factor. Can donate to: B+, B-, AB+, AB-. Can receive from: B-, O-

5. AB+
Has both A and B antigens and Rh factor. Universal recipient. Can donate to: AB+ only. Can receive from: All blood types

6. AB-
Has both A and B antigens, no Rh factor. Can donate to: AB+, AB-. Can receive from: A-, B-, AB-, O-

7. O+
No A or B antigens, but has Rh factor. Can donate to: A+, B+, AB+, O+. Can receive from: O+, O-

8. O-
No A/B antigens or Rh factor. Universal donor. Can donate to: All blood types. Can receive from: O- only
