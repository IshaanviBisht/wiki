üå¶Ô∏è Explaining the Water Cycle

The water cycle is the natural process by which water moves continuously through the environment. It begins when the Sun heats up water in oceans, rivers, and lakes, causing it to evaporate and turn into water vapor. Plants also release water vapor into the air through a process called transpiration. This vapor rises into the atmosphere, cools down, and condenses to form clouds. When the clouds become heavy, the water falls back to the Earth as precipitation, such as rain or snow. This water then collects in rivers, lakes, oceans, or seeps into the ground as groundwater. From there, the cycle starts all over again, helping to maintain life and balance in nature.
